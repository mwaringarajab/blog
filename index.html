Introduction

In an era where digital skills are the currency of the future, access to quality tech education is more crucial than ever. The Power Learn Project (PLP) is an initiative dedicated to bridging the digital skills gap in Africa by equipping young people with essential programming and software development knowledge. This initiative is not just about teaching code; it's about empowering individuals to build solutions, create employment opportunities, and drive digital transformation in their communities.

What is the Power Learn Project?

The Power Learn Project is a Pan-African social impact initiative that provides accessible and affordable tech education to youth across the continent. Its core mission is to create a 1 million software developers talent pipeline in Africa by offering free or subsidized training programs. PLP leverages a mobile-first, self-paced learning approach, making it convenient for learners to access courses from anywhere. With a strong focus on practical skills, mentorship, and community building, the project is shaping the next generation of African tech leaders.

How to get involved

If you’re an aspiring developer or a tech enthusiast looking to upskill, the Power Learn Project provides an excellent opportunity to learn and grow. You can sign up for courses on their website, participate in their events, and engage with the vibrant PLP community. Additionally, organizations, tech companies, and philanthropists can support PLP through funding, mentorship programs, or providing job opportunities for graduates.PLP offers curriculum that include:

  • Python programming
  • Web development (HTML, CSS, JavaScript)
  • Databases and backend development
  • Mobile app development
  • Cybersecurity and cloud computing
